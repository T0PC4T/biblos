ποδ-ήρης , ες,
A.reaching to the feet, πέπλοι, χιτὼν π., a robe that falls over the feet, E.Ba.833, X.Cyr.6.4.2, Paus.5.19.6, etc. (later ποδήρης alone (sc. χιτών) of the High Priest's robe, LXXEx.25.6, Aristeas96); π. ἀσπίς large shield which covered the body down to the feet, X.An.1.8.9, Cyr.6.2.10: Com., “πώγων π. καθεῖται” Plu.2.52c.
2. ναῦς π. a ship with feet, i.e. oars, Hsch., Eust.1515.29; στῦλος π. a firmly based pillar, A.Ag.898.
3. τὰ π. parts about the feet, feet, ib.1594. (-ήρης perh. from -α_ρης, cf. Arc. pr. n. Ποδάρης.）
